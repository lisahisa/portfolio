Process

Process

1

Research

Before going into creative development, research is conducted to understand the project better. Research can be divided into two sections: literary research—compiling information about the client, industry, and target audience—and visual research—drawing inspiration from other successful design solutions.

2

Ideate

The ideation process begins with a “brain dump,” where the problem is tackled from various perspectives. This typically takes the form of mind maps and rough thumbnail sketches, exploring a diverse range of solutions and always referencing the research that was conducted.

3

Develop

The numerous solutions that were created in the Ideate step are then narrowed down to a single direction, determining the best option that achieves the project’s objectives. At this stage, decisions about color usage, typography, layouts, and imagery are made based on the visual research, visualizing how the final result could look.

4

Revise

Once a testable prototype is developed, it goes through a series of checkpoints, receiving feedback from the client as well as user groups. The design is refined and finalized until it meets the project’s objectives and satisfies the client’s needs.
